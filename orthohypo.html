Orthostatic Hypotension
Definition
  • a fall in systolic blood pressure of at least 20 mm Hg or diastolic blood pressure of at least 10 mm Hg within 3 minutes after standing (AAFP)
  • However, SCI patients can often become symptomatic even when sitting.
Risk Factors
  • injuries T6 and higher
  • complete injuries
Treatment
  • Reposition — decrease seat-back angle in a recliner wheelchair from near supine gradually to full upright as tolerated
  • Elastic stocking/abdominal binder/ace wrap LE
  • Exercises –use of tilt table
  • Fluid resuscitation: increase fluid intake
  • Pharmacological
    • salt tablets
    • midodrine: used 1 hour prior to desired effect
    • fludrocortisone
